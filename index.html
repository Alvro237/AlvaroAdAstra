Hello there! Welcome to another aerospace engineer's site

This is my personal website dedicated to showcasing my work and projects in the aerospace field. It contains a bit about my background, and most of my interests. The real motivation behind this page is to keep track of my experience and share what I have learned through the years. Despite this being for my personal use, anyone is free to roam this page and use its tools.

About me

If you have reached this far, I should present myself. I just graduated from my bachelor in aerospace engineering, which means I am oficially an engineer. My college years have been the most complicated yet amusing years of my life. Each challenge brought the best of me and my classmates, and the lessons I learned will follow me throughout my carrer. You may be wondering where it may lead. Well, me too. However, I have always had an objective in mind, and the name of this page could give you a little hint. "Per aspera ad astra", which translates to "Through hardships to the stars". Of course, this sentence is not mine, but I couldn't have expressed it better. Indeed, this journey started when I decided to become an astronaut, over 15 years ago. Over time, I have met some brilliant minds, with similar interests and passions. I have discovered new branches of engineering, and my reasons to pursue this carrer have shifted. When I was young, I wanted to be in space, feel the zero gravity and see earth from a unique perspective. Later, I became aware that what pushes me further is the human desire to explore the unknown. Over the years, I have learned to crave a great challenge, and enjoy the feeling of completing it. Now, the will to contribute to enhancing humanity's knowledge and secure our place among the stars drives me forward.

However, there is still much work to do, and my mission is far from being over. As you may have noticed, I am not an astronaut (yet, I hope). But my passion for engineering is still there, and thus I still push forward learning more about rocket propulsion, astrophysics, satellites, launches, space vehicles... and with this page, programming! Because no matter how incredible the destination may be, the aerospace journey still fascinates me day after day, and reminds me there are discoveries yet to be made in every single domain of science.

My Experience

  • 2024-2025

    Simulations Engineer @Madrid Space Europe

    • Madrid Space is a consultant company for the Space Cluster. They solve thermal, structural and fluid problems, no matter how complex they are!
    • I learned how to perform three types of simulations:
      • CFD with Hypermesh CFD and OpenFOAM
      • Thermal analyses with ESATAN
      • Structural analyses with Hypermesh (FEM)
    • It all started as an internship, but after 4 months I had a contract to work as a junior engineer!
